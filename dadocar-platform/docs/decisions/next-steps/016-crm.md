# 016 — CRM: customers, payments, profit & spend tracking

- **Status**: Open
- **Effort**: 2-3 weeks
- **Depends on**: [002](002-customer-model-multi-tenancy.md) (customer model)
- **Blocks**: running the business as a paid product; informs [017](017-paywall-self-serve-provisioning.md)
- **Related**: [0007](../0007-webclient-productization.md) (app-local tenants today)

## Why

Today there is **no system to manage customers as a business**. The webclient has app-local `users` + per-tenant data ([0007](../0007-webclient-productization.md)), but nothing tracks:

- **Who the customers are** as billable accounts (company, contact, plan, status) — distinct from login users.
- **Payment tracking** — what was charged, paid, overdue, refunded; MRR.
- **Profit tracking** — revenue per customer minus the **vendor cost** of their queries (each Molicar/CheckTudo call is a paid upstream request).
- **Spend tracking** — our outgoing cost to vendors (Molicar, CheckTudo, Anthropic, Azure) per customer and in aggregate.

Without it we can't tell which customers are profitable, can't reconcile vendor invoices, and can't make pricing decisions.

## Scope

In:

- A **CRM data model** (likely the platform `customers` model in Cosmos from [002](002-customer-model-multi-tenancy.md), extended): account, contacts, plan/tier, status, billing identifiers (Stripe customer/subscription), lifecycle dates.
- **Payment ledger**: invoices/charges synced from Stripe ([003](003-stripe-and-provisioning.md)) — amount, status, period, paid_at.
- **Query-cost attribution**: per consult, record the vendor cost (Molicar / CheckTudo product price / Anthropic tokens) against the customer. Source: query events + a vendor price table.
- **Profit view**: per customer and global — revenue − vendor spend − infra allocation; margin; queries/month.
- **Admin CRM UI** (Master-only): list customers, drill into payments, usage, profit/margin, spend.
- Decide build-vs-buy: a lightweight in-app CRM (our own tables + admin pages) vs. integrating an external CRM/billing tool (e.g. Stripe Billing dashboards, a SaaS CRM). Recommend starting in-app, backed by Stripe for payments.

Out:

- Sales pipeline / marketing automation (lead nurturing) — not needed at this stage.
- Double-entry accounting — track for decisions, not as the books of record.

## Approach

1. Land [002](002-customer-model-multi-tenancy.md) (customer entity) first; the CRM extends it.
2. Add a **vendor price table** (Molicar per-call, each CheckTudo product's price, Anthropic per-1k-tokens) and stamp a `cost_estimate` on every query event / consult row.
3. Sync Stripe charges/invoices into a payment ledger ([003](003-stripe-and-provisioning.md) webhook handler).
4. Build the Master-only CRM pages in the webclient (or a dedicated admin app): customers list → per-customer payments, usage, profit/margin.
5. Wire registration/provisioning ([017](017-paywall-self-serve-provisioning.md)) to create the CRM customer record automatically.

## Success criteria

- Every customer has a CRM record with plan, status, and Stripe linkage.
- For any month, see per-customer **revenue, vendor spend, and profit/margin**, and the global totals.
- Each consult is attributed a vendor cost; sum reconciles (±) against the vendor invoice.
- A new paid sign-up automatically appears in the CRM.
